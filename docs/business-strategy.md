# Business Strategy

## Initial Positioning

GeoWorkbench should initially position itself as:

> An AI-assisted geological workflow platform for coal exploration and mine geology.

The product should emphasize:

- productivity
- workflow acceleration
- interpretation assistance
- geological data organization

rather than claiming autonomous geological intelligence.

---

# Initial Market Entry

## Recommended Entry Point

Start with:

- exploration teams
- mine geology departments
- geological consultants
- coal block technical teams

Especially organizations using:

- Excel-heavy workflows
- manual reporting
- fragmented geological datasets

---

# Early Value Proposition

The strongest early value is likely:

- reduced manual interpretation work
- faster reporting
- cleaner datasets
- improved consistency
- centralized geological workflows

---

# Why This Opportunity Exists

Current mining/geology software often suffers from:

- outdated UX
- disconnected systems
- weak automation
- limited AI integration
- poor workflow usability

This creates opportunity for AI-native workflow platforms.

---

# Recommended Go-To-Market Strategy

## Phase 1

Pilot with a small number of geologists.

Focus:

- workflow validation
- feedback loops
- data structure learning
- UX iteration

---

## Phase 2

Deploy operational MVPs inside selected projects.

Focus:

- measurable productivity gains
- report acceleration
- seam interpretation workflows

---

## Phase 3

Expand into broader geological intelligence platform.

Potential:

- enterprise deployments
- cross-project analytics
- geological knowledge systems
- AI-enhanced exploration workflows

---

# Strategic Moat

The moat should come from:

- geological workflow integration
- accumulated correction data
- coal-specific ontology
- customer adaptation
- institutional geological memory

This is likely stronger long-term than individual ML models.
